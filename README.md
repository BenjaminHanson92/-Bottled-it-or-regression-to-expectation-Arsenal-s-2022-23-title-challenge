This is an analysis of Arsenal's unexpected position in the 2022-23 Premier League standings. They had a big lead at the top of the table going into the final 9 games, but failed to win the title. I take an objective look at the evidence - including preseason expectations and performance levels throughout the season relative to results -  to determine if there is merit to the media narrative that Arsenal collapsed under pressure.

CSV files downloaded from https://www.football-data.co.uk/
Comprehensive match stats (shots, corners, cards etc) and pre-game betting lines for every game of the 2022-2023 season.
File sizes of between 170KB and 270KB.

Data cleaning: 	- Checked for duplicates or missing values			
		- Replaced abbreviations for easier understanding	

I created a new Excel table by filtering for Arsenal's first 10 home games and first 10 away games. I deleted all but the columns I needed and added new ones (columns I, J, K). Simple SUM function used to calculate totals.

I also took data from Fivethirtyeight.com for preseason predictions, and in-season XG stats from footballxg.com

I chose to create a power point and spreadsheet to showcase my full analysis and conclusions.

Statistical formulas and functions used are explained in the spreadsheet.
